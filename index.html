1. Understanding Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD)

What is SAD?

Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD) is a type of depression that occurs during specific seasons, most commonly in the fall and winter months. Symptoms may include low energy, difficulty concentrating, changes in sleep patterns, and feelings of sadness or hopelessness.

At MyndWorks Psychiatry, the team understands the unique challenges of SAD and offers tailored treatment options to help patients regain balance and well-being.

2. Treatment Options for SAD at MyndWorks

Comprehensive and Personalized Care

MyndWorks Psychiatry provides a range of evidence-based treatments for SAD, including:

  • Light Therapy: Exposure to bright light to help regulate mood and energy levels.
  • Medication Management: Prescribing antidepressants or other medications as needed.
  • Therapy: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) to address negative thought patterns and develop coping strategies.
  • Holistic Approaches: Recommendations for lifestyle changes, such as exercise and nutrition, to support overall mental health.
